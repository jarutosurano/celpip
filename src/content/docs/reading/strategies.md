## Skimming vs Scanning

### Skimming (Get the Big Picture)
- Read first and last paragraphs
- Read first sentence of each paragraph
- Look at headings, bold text, lists
- **Goal:** Understand main idea in 1-2 minutes

### Scanning (Find Specific Info)
- Know what you're looking for
- Move eyes quickly over text
- Look for keywords, numbers, names
- **Goal:** Locate answer in seconds

## Reading Strategy by Part

### Part 1: Correspondence
1. Identify sender and recipient
2. Determine the purpose immediately
3. Note any action items or requests
4. Watch for tone (formal, casual, urgent)

### Part 2: Diagram
1. Study the diagram FIRST
2. Understand labels and legends
3. Read text while referring to diagram
4. Match text descriptions to visual elements

### Part 3: Information
1. Read questions before the passage
2. Identify keywords in questions
3. Scan passage for those keywords
4. Read surrounding sentences for answer

### Part 4: Viewpoints
1. Identify the author's main argument
2. Note supporting points
3. Watch for contrast words (however, but, although)
4. Distinguish facts from opinions

## Time-Saving Tips

### Don't Read Word-by-Word
- Read in phrases, not individual words
- Your brain can process groups of words
- Practice expanding your visual span

### Don't Subvocalize
- Stop "saying" words in your head
- It slows you down significantly
- Let your eyes do the work

### Skip and Return
- If stuck on a question, mark it and move on
- Come back with fresh eyes
- Don't let one question eat your time

## Common Mistakes

:::danger[Avoid These]
- Reading the entire passage before questions
- Spending 5+ minutes on one question
- Second-guessing correct answers
- Not reading all answer options
- Missing "NOT" or "EXCEPT" in questions
:::

## Practice Exercise

1. Set a timer for 5 minutes
2. Read a news article
3. Cover the article
4. Write down 5 main points
5. Check your accuracy

Repeat daily to improve retention and speed.
